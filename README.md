Hi, I'm Yung Hsuan. An analytics practitioner with a background in economics, risk management, and e-commerce operations.

I didn't come to analytics through a technical track. My undergraduate training was in economics, focused on how people and systems make decisions under uncertainty. That lens stayed with me when I moved into e-commerce, where I spent time managing commercial operations and realized that most business decisions are only as good as the data informing them. That's what eventually pushed me toward a more formal analytics foundation.

I'm currently completing my **MSBA at the Carlson School of Management, University of Minnesota**. At Carlson, I've worked on applied projects across construction, transportation, and data infrastructure — where the problem is real and the stakeholder actually needs an answer. I've found that what makes analysis useful isn't sophistication; it's whether it reaches the right person in a form they can act on.

When I'm not working, I'm usually at a coffee shop with a book, or out on a long walk.
